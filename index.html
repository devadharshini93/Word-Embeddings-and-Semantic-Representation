Word Embeddings & Semantic Representation

Understanding how machines interpret human language

📌 Introduction

In Natural Language Processing (NLP), machines need a way to understand human language. Word embeddings provide a powerful solution by converting words into numerical vectors that capture meaning and relationships.

🔍 What are Word Embeddings?

Word embeddings are dense vector representations of words. They capture semantic meaning, allowing similar words to have similar vector representations.

Example: "king" and "queen" are closer than "king" and "car"
king → [0.9, 0.8, 0.7]
queen → [0.9, 0.85, 0.75]

⚠️ Limitations of One-Hot Encoding

  • Does not capture meaning
  • High dimensional vectors
  • Sparse representation

🚀 Popular Techniques

Word2Vec

Uses neural networks (CBOW & Skip-gram)

GloVe

Combines global & local statistics

FastText

Handles subwords & rare words

🧠 Semantic Representation

Semantic representation allows machines to understand relationships between words.

king - man + woman ≈ queen

📊 Applications

Sentiment Analysis
Chatbots
Machine Translation
Search Engines

✅ Conclusion

Word embeddings revolutionized NLP by enabling machines to understand context and meaning. They are the foundation of modern AI models like BERT and GPT.
